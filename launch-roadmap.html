1 · the ten calls.

Decisions already made (or forced by facts). Everything else in this document hangs off these.

  1. Four SKUs, hydration, one drop — statement 01: cleanser · essence · serum (hero) · moisturizer. Confirmed lineup in §4.
  2. Packaging = UA Packaging (Jiangmen, China), 10,000 components per SKU. The 10K tier is the rational buy: it prices at roughly half the 5K tier, so the second 5,000 units cost only ~$8–9K more across the line. Quote is expired — refresh first.
  3. First fill ≈ 2,500 units per SKU (10,000+ total) at Pravada, customizations quoted as custom runs. Email #1 is SENT (custom packaging at ~10K/product, serum cleanup, non-comedogenic cream, lighter-ceramide options, MOQs + price implications). Final fill size is set when their numbers land; the follow-up asks ride the reply or the call (§14, item 1).
  4. Components ≠ filled goods. Bottles don't expire; filled product does (typically 24–36 months unopened — confirm per SKU). Surplus components (~7,500/SKU) are stored and become refill runs. Never fill more than the demand forecast supports.
  5. Plan-of-record launch: Friday, January 15, 2027 — peak dry-skin season, no heroics, ~3 weeks of true slack. Stretch: Dec 4, 2026, decided Jul 14 when Pravada's lead times land. Slip is accepted; a broken launch is not. Full logic in §2.
  6. Two go/no-go legal gates before any big money moves: attorney trademark clearance (house mark + all 7 product names, opinion due Jul 24) and Pravada's written yes on filling customer-supplied components (due Jul 14).
  7. Names: the statement-name system (idiom + period + functional descriptor). Recommended set: come clean. / ice breaker. / deep end. / smooth talker. — Thomas approves, attorney clears, then they lock. §5.
  8. Positioning: the brand that shows its receipts. Every number sourced. All mock reviews, invented panels ("96% · n=120"), and placeholder ratings get deleted before launch — an honesty brand cannot ship fiction. §8–§9.
  9. Paid ads never lead. Organic founder content + waitlist ("the brief") + review panel + seeding are the engine; paid is branded-search defense only. §9.
  10. Year-one revenue expectation stays $50–100K even though inventory is sized bigger. The inventory is a deliberate multi-year capital bet — it must not eat the marketing budget or the cash floor. §3 and §11.

3.4 break-even and the elephant

The elephant, named: filling 10,000 units against a realistic launch window of 100–500 orders and a realistic first year of $50–100K means most filled inventory sits 18+ months. Filled skincare typically carries 24–36 months unopened shelf life — get the per-SKU number from Pravada's stability docs before the fill size is final. At the midpoint (~$165K deployed, ~$48 average contribution per order) break-even is roughly 2,900–3,400 orders ≈ $200–235K revenue — a mid/late-2027 event at base trajectory, not a 2026 one.

This is survivable if it's deliberate: the components (no expiry) are a cheap multi-year position; the fill is the perishable bet. The protective moves, in order of power: ① size the first fill to what Pravada's tier pricing makes sensible (1,000–2,500/SKU) and keep the rest as components; ② confirm shelf life per SKU in writing; ③ ring-fence a cash floor (≥$15–20K) for marketing and operations — a warehouse of product with no audience budget is how launches die quietly; ④ re-forecast fill #2 from real sell-through at day 60, not from hope.

6.3 decoration + art (this is what gates the PO)

  • Decoration = UA screen print + spray coat (system rule: 1-color front + 1-color back; no stickered labels; matt tube finish $0.05/unit; cap emboss "ily." requires one-time tooling — get the number).   • Recommended exception to the no-labels rule (decide by jul 24): screen-print the front/brand layer only, and have Pravada apply a printed back label carrying INCI + regulatory copy. Cost is trivial; it converts a "reprint 10,000 bottles in China" compliance error into a "reprint labels in Florida" one, and lets formula tweaks or claim edits happen without scrapping components. 9 · marketing.

One system, three layers: receipts (trust), statement drops (acquisition), the routine (economics). Thesis: earn trust with receipts, launch in statements, sell the routine.

9.1 receipts — the credibility engine

  • The launch's best stories already happened in the formulation work: we removed witch hazel and lavender from our own hero before selling it; we swapped the clogging emollient for squalane. The edits are the receipts — no competitor can tell this story about our formulas.
  • Formats: "read the label with us" founder videos · the refuse list (one refused ingredient per post) · ingredient SEO pages (multi-molecular hyaluronic acid, oat milk cleanser sensitive skin, non-comedogenic peptide moisturizer, essence vs toner) · the parked "named." template as the recurring ingredient-hero format.
  • Rules: every number sourced, source directly beneath the claim; smaller true numbers beat bigger fake ones; knowledgeable friend, never the callout account; never name competitors.

9.2 statement drops — the launch architecture

  • Sell numbered statements, not a catalog: "statement 01: hydration." Labels carry statement 01 — hydration. as a system line; the routine poem rides a business-card-grade insert.
  • The waitlist is the campaign. Everything funnels to one list, "the brief." Target 2,000+ emails by launch (5,000 is the stretch). VIP early access 24–48h before public.
  • Cadence: founder-led TikTok/IG 4–6×/week starting mid-July — the 60–90-day organic runway is why Jan 15 converts better than a rushed December. Name reveals one per week in period-stack style during the countdown month; drop-day post is just the poem.
  • Professional credibility: the review panel (Zach + 2–3 estheticians/derm-adjacent pros) trials finished goods in December; quoted with permission at launch. This replaces every placeholder review.
  • Seeding: 300–500 units to nano/micro creators, shipped the week stock lands in Minneapolis ($4–10K all-in). Gift, don't pay — at this size paid sponsorship math doesn't work.

9.3 the routine — retention + economics

  • Hero offer: the 4-SKU routine bundle ("the full statement.", $136 placeholder vs $156 separates). Serum is the single-SKU entry door; flows upgrade serum buyers to the routine.
  • Subscribe & save on 60-day cadence; replenishment copy "keep your word." Repeat rate, subscription take-rate, and review velocity are the proof pack an eventual Sephora conversation needs — DTC data IS the retail pitch.

11 · realistic expectations.

  • Waitlist math: waitlists convert at under 5%. 2,000–5,000 emails → 100–500 launch-window orders TOTAL (not per SKU). That's a good launch for a bootstrapped brand with no prior audience.
  • Launch-window revenue: 100–500 orders at ~$60–90 AOV ≈ $7–40K. The inventory does not sell out in January — it isn't supposed to.
  • Year one: Beauty-Independent benchmarks for a solo bootstrapped founder: $50K is a good year, $100K is fantastic. That expectation does NOT change because the inventory got bigger — the inventory is a multi-year position (§3.4), and the plan must survive on year-one revenue without touching the cash floor.
  • What compounds: repeat rate on the 60-day cycle, subscription take-rate, review velocity, and email list growth. Those four numbers are the business (and the future Sephora pitch). Vanity spikes are not.
  • Success checkpoints: day 30 — 150+ orders cumulative, 3.5%+ site CVR from the warm list, first organic UGC. Day 90 — 400+ orders, 15%+ repeat/subscription attach on early cohorts, one content format reliably producing. Day 365 — $50–100K revenue, 2,500+ list, fill #2 sized from data. If day-90 numbers are far under, the fix is audience and offer — not more inventory.
  • The pattern to guard against (named with love): exploration-as-procrastination. Five site directions, ~40 packaging renders, and a 14-day-unsent email preceded this roadmap. The system is now decided. From here, every open question has an owner and a date — re-opening settled decisions costs launch weeks.
