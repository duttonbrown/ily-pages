material options to consider

ua publishes eight sustainability paths. the table below covers the ones most relevant to this 4-sku regimen — what each is, the trade-off versus our default virgin-plastic / glass spec, and where to read more on ua's site.

material path what it is trade-off vs default read more
pcr (mechanical) recycled plastic content, post-consumer source slight color tint, modest cost premium (~10–20%); fine for matte/coated bodies where tint is hidden pcr article
chemical pcr depolymerized + repolymerized recycled feedstock looks/performs like virgin; no tint penalty; higher cost than mechanical pcr chemical pcr article
mono-material single-polymer construction (e.g. all-pp tube + pp cap) easier curbside recycle; pump/dispenser choices constrained — relevant for the cleanser tube mono-material pump
aluminum metal vessel, infinitely recyclable denting risk; different decoration palette; ua now offers an aluminum airless (relevant to the moisturizer) aluminum overview · aluminum airless
refillable replaceable inner cartridge, consumer keeps the outer higher unit cost, lower lifetime cost; needs consumer education; strong fit for the airless tier refillable advantages
glass (frosted/coated) weight + perceived premium; recyclable; the serum is already this shipping weight + breakage risk; not a replacement for the cleanser tube but worth keeping for the serum tier glass catalog
recommended path for ily: mechanical or chemical pcr on the toner and moisturizer plastic bodies; glass on the serum (already speced); mono-material on the cleanser tube for the recyclability story without the tint penalty. defer aluminum airless and refillable to v2 unless the team wants to lead with the sustainability story at launch.

worth adding (quote these)

add · de-risk pricing

confirm cap emboss tooling per diameter

get ua to quote exact tooling per cap diameter. assumed ~$2,500 each but it's quote-by-design. if 3 of the 4 skus share a diameter, the one-time spend could drop ~$5k.

add · sensory upgrade

soft-touch finish on the airless moisturizer

capabilities doc lists "frosted soft touch — enhances sensory experience." +$0.05–0.08 / unit. moves the airless from "looks premium" to "feels premium when held" — what tatcha and drunk elephant lala retro do. → frosted soft touch on ua

add · sustainability story

pcr or chemical pcr on plastic bodies (toner, moisturizer)

10–25% premium per capabilities doc. ~$0.15–0.45 added per unit. real (not badge-y) sustainability story without changing the look. let the material speak. → pcr · chemical pcr · 5 sustainable material paths

add · use experience

reducer on the toner

capabilities §8 calls this out specifically for toners. ~$0.03–0.05 / unit. prevents over-pour failure mode that kills toner repurchase rates. → reducers & dispensers

add · option to evaluate

refillable inner cartridge for the moisturizer

complexity premium and consumer-education load, but lowers lifetime cost and aligns with the airless tier's premium positioning. worth quoting alongside the standard airless to compare. → refillable advantages

refuse / remove

cut · false savings

don't add 2nd or 3rd silkscreen colors

doubles silkscreen cost ($0.072 → $0.144 per face). the ingredient-led typographic hierarchy should do the work — additional colors don't earn back.

cut · off-brand

no varnish, metallic, or hologram

varnish at $0.20/unit adds cost while subtracting brand value. brand position is "frosted/matte never glossy." don't quote.

cut · won't pay back

no custom shape molds

at 5–10k volumes the $2,500+ blow mold or pricier injection mold doesn't earn back. stock shapes are fine for launch; revisit at 100k+ when ua waives the mold fee.

open decisions before placing the order

  1. variant pricing parity. the notion notes flag that the actual variants we want — pl001-2382 (not 3493), pl042-60249 heavy-wall (not 71350), pl184-kh30-24 (not 77) — are not in the quote. send ua the four product urls and confirm whether quoted pricing applies before locking.
  2. cleanser sizing. formulation page targets 150–180ml; round-dia80 is 130ml ≈ 4.4oz. the selected tube is too small for the stated cleanser target. either accept 4oz/130ml as final, or re-spec to a 150ml tube (tu017-d45, tu039-d50, or pl008-7138-5 all available at $0.43–$0.52 per unit). resolve this first.
  3. cap emboss tooling. confirm with ua whether tooling fee scales per diameter or per design, and whether shared cap diameters across skus share a tool.
  4. setup fee math. at 10k per sku the $500 setup is waived. if any sku pilots at 5k, model the +$500 + ~$0.04–0.06 silkscreen tier delta + ~$0.06–0.10 coating tier delta before locking volumes.

bottom line

the 4-sku regimen lands at ~$5.30 in packaging cogs to deliver a $94–$137 retail line — 4–6% packaging cogs, sitting cleanly inside the premium-skincare norm and right next to laneige and drunk elephant.

the aesthetic spec — matte/frosted, function-color body, 1-color silkscreen, cap emboss, shadow grey accent on pumps — is a legitimately premium look built almost entirely on ua's standard catalog with no custom tooling, except the cap emboss, which is the one tooling spend worth committing to.

the single biggest risk is the cleanser tube being too small for the stated target size. resolve that before anything else.
