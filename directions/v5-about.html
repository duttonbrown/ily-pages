the why

we started iloveyouth because most "hydrating" skincare isn't. drying alcohols, witch hazel, fragrance — the things that strip the skin barrier — show up in moisturizers, toners, and serums marketed as the opposite of what they do.

we built six products around three ingredients with decades of research behind them. niacinamide. ceramides. hyaluronic acid. nothing in our formulas undoes the good stuff. that's the entire brand.

the voice

five pillars. one voice.

how we say what we say. reviewed for every campaign, every label, every line.
01

honest, not preachy.

we say what the product does. no jargon, no empty promises. if it hydrates, we say it hydrates. we don't dress it up in clinical language or marketing superlatives.

02

warmly confident.

we know our products work. we don't oversell or hedge. we're matter-of-fact about efficacy — the formula speaks.

03

casually human.

conversational, never clinical. like a knowledgeable friend, not a dermatologist, not a sales rep. room for personality, warmth, and the occasional dry observation.

04

pro-youth, anti-hype.

we celebrate healthy, hydrated skin at any age. never fear-based "anti-aging" language. we're not about fighting your face — we're about taking care of it.

05

ingredient-forward.

ingredients are our credibility. we name them, say what they do in plain terms, and move on. no jargon walls. no proprietary-blend mystery.

∞

the through-line.

if you wouldn't say it to a friend's face, we don't write it. the gut check that survives every campaign.

the formula

what we refuse to put in.

scroll →
the dehydrator

×drying alcohols

they evaporate fast and feel "fresh" — and leave skin drier than they found it. there's no version of this we'd ship.

the irritant

×witch hazel

it strips the barrier, especially on sensitive skin. there are gentler astringents. we don't need either.

the trigger

×fragrance

a top reactivity flag. nothing in our formula needs to smell like anything. it just has to work.

the bulk

×fillers

if it's not earning its place in the formula, it's not in the formula. ingredient transparency starts here.

the breakout cause

×comedogenic oils

if it clogs pores, it doesn't belong in a hydration product. acne-prone skin gets the same formula.

the legacy issue

×parabens / sulfates

we don't lead with "clean" as marketing. but if a category-standard ingredient irritates skin, we leave it out.

the horizon

we're patient. the formula isn't.

iloveyouth is direct-to-consumer. always will be, in some channel mix. we're building toward retail with sephora as the target — not because the brand needs that validation, but because the customer who hasn't found us yet should be able to.
